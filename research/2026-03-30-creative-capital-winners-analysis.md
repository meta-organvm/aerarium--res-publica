# Creative Capital "Emerging Fields" / Technology Winners — Pattern Analysis (2026-03-30)

## Source
Perplexity Pro deep research query, 2026-03-30

## Key Finding
Creative Capital does NOT have a distinct "Emerging Fields" category. Technology-engaged work
is funded through Visual Arts and Performing Arts categories, with "Technology" as an explicit
sub-discipline option starting with the 2027 Open Call.

## Funded Projects — Systems Thinking / Computational / Governance

### Rachel Rossin — "On the Poetics of Wireless Fidelity (WiFi)" (2024, Technology/Visual Arts)
- Self-powered sculptural objects harvest wasted WiFi energy
- Microcontrollers + organic LED screens + AI systems
- Framing: "human sovereignty" + infrastructure critique
- **Pattern: invisible systems made visible through autonomous sculptural objects**

### Alice Bucknell — "Earth Engine" (2025, Technology)
- Game that makes Earth the player, humans the NPCs
- Real-time climate projection data → in-game ecological assets
- Rejects "more data = more reality" — critiques computational determinism
- **Pattern: systems-thinking that interrogates systems-thinking itself**

### Shayla Blatchford — "Anti-Uranium Mapping Project" (2025, Technology)
- Data visualization + historical documentation + activist testimony
- Feeds data to AI with "correct information" about environmental racism
- Exists as website, educational resource, activist tool simultaneously
- **Pattern: platform as governance structure for collective action**

### Morehshin Allahyari — "The Remaining Signs of Future Centuries" (2025, Technology)
- Islamic Golden Era innovations through "anti-colonial queer feminist lens"
- 3D simulation, video, sculpture, digital fabrication
- **Pattern: computational methods recovering overlooked knowledge systems**

### Brian House — "Macrophones" (Technology/Environmental)
- Cold War listening tech + ML to convert infrasound to audible frequencies
- Audio AR positioning listeners within distant soundscapes
- Installed in old-growth forests, Arctic tundra, urban centers
- **Pattern: distributed listening as environmental governance**

### Rashaad Newsome — "I Come As One, But I Stand As One Thousand" (2025, Technology/Performance)
- AI choreographs 1,000 LED drones + live performers + orchestra
- Human-AI choreography interpenetrating
- **Pattern: post-human collective emergence as artistic form**

### Avram Finkelstein — "Corpus/Fluxus" (2024, Visual Arts/Social Practice)
- Interrogates "public-ness" vs "shared" space accessibility
- Translucent vellums activated by viewers' presence
- **Pattern: institutional critique of accessibility frameworks**

### Jeremy Toussaint-Baptiste — "There, Eyes Were Watching" (2025, Visual Arts)
- "Monument by subtraction" — demolish plantation-turned-B&B
- Replace with monument serving Indigenous communities
- **Pattern: counter-institutional monuments through subtraction**

## Five Patterns for ORGANVM Application

1. **Double rigor**: Artistic excellence AND conceptual depth simultaneously.
   Not "I use code" but "code is specifically necessary for this investigation."

2. **Interrogate, don't celebrate**: Systems-thinking as critique, not decoration.
   The eight-organ model questions how one person can operate at institutional scale.

3. **Distributed agency**: Move beyond individual authorship toward collective emergence.
   ORGANVM distributes creative agency across 8 organs + AI conductors + governance.

4. **Grounded in specifics**: Abstract systems anchored in particular places/struggles.
   The consulting practice, the client work, the community — not just the theory.

5. **Accessibility and public engagement**: Work that communicates beyond art-world.
   Building-in-public, open governance, the ORGANVM as observable system.

## Application Language That Works
- Identify a specific systemic PROBLEM your work investigates
- Explain why computational/systems methods are NECESSARY (not decorative)
- Articulate a "catalytic moment" in your practice
- Balance ambition with feasibility — show you can execute
- Frame: "the eight-organ model IS the artwork, the governance IS the formal innovation"
